# Method 03 — How Might We Questions

> Derived from synthesis themes. Each HMW frames an opportunity for Method 4 brainstorming without prescribing a solution.

## From Theme 1: Two Modes of Fun

**HMW-01** — How might we design the Commander's triage loop so every dispatch decision feels meaningful, even when the city is clearly going to fall?

**HMW-02** — How might we design the kaiju roster so each archetype's role in a combo is obvious from its visual design alone, without needing a guide?

**HMW-03** — How might we ensure both roles feel complete and satisfying in 90 seconds — neither running out of things to do nor feeling overwhelmed?

---

## From Theme 2: The Room Is the Third Mechanic

**HMW-04** — How might we design combo mechanics so that verbal in-room coordination ("attack NOW!") is the natural and sufficient way to trigger them?

**HMW-05** — How might we make the Commander's screen watchable as a spectator sport, so eliminated players and observers stay engaged between rounds?

**HMW-06** — How might we reduce session setup friction (joining, role selection, starting a round) to under 60 seconds so the social momentum never stalls?

---

## From Theme 3: Managed Retreat as Emotional Arc

**HMW-07** — How might we present the evacuation score at match end so it feels like an achievement rather than a consolation prize?

**HMW-08** — How might we design the high score leaderboard entry (initials + score) to create a moment of social ceremony at the end of each Commander round?

**HMW-09** — How might we give kaiju players a sense of personal contribution within the collective win — so "we destroyed the city" feels like "I mattered in that win"?

---

## From Theme 4: Legibility Over Depth

**HMW-10** — How might we communicate rocket launch readiness and risk (launch site threatened) at a glance, without adding UI complexity to the Commander dashboard?

**HMW-11** — How might we design the kaiju ability system so a player understands what their kaiju does within the first 15 seconds of their first match?

**HMW-12** — How might we make the first round of a session work as a tutorial — so watching or playing it once is enough to understand the game?
