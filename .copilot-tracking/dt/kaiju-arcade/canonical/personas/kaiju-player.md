title: "Persona — The Kaiju Player"
---

### Description

A Kaiju player controls a single leviathan advancing on the city. There are one to four of them per match, each on their own device (phone or tablet). They pick from a roster of distinct kaiju archetypes — each with different normal and special attacks — and have unlimited respawns via the INSERT COIN flash. Die, press a key, return to the map edge. Individually fragile but collectively unstoppable.

### User Goal

Destroy the city base before the 90-second clock runs out. Coordinate timing and attacks with other kaiju — including combo sequences that amplify damage — to overwhelm the Commander's defenses. Feel like a force of nature.

### User Needs

- Immediate, readable feedback: HP, abilities, cooldowns, base distance — all at a glance on a mobile screen
- A respawn flow that feels dramatic but fast — INSERT COIN flash, press key, back in action within seconds
- A kaiju archetype whose role in a combo is obvious from its visual design, discoverable in the first 15 seconds of play
- Clear signal when the city base is taking damage and when a combo lands — this is how they know they're winning
- Enough variety across archetypes to make roster choice feel meaningful across multiple sessions

### User Mindset

Kaiju players want to feel powerful and chaotic. Coordination happens verbally in the room — "everyone attack NOW" — not through complex in-game menus. They enjoy the improvised emergence of a combo that works out, and the spectacle of watching the Commander scramble. Dying repeatedly is fine; the respawn must feel exciting, not punishing. They are not trying to outthink the Commander — they are trying to overwhelm them. Short rounds mean they can try a different archetype next time.
