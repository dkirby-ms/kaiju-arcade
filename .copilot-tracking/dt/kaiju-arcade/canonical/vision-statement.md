## Vision Statement

Build a short-form, BYOD local multiplayer arcade game where one player commands a city's last defense while one to four others pilot unstoppable kaiju — a consequence-free simulation of asymmetric incident command where the only question is how many you can save before the city falls.

### Why This Matters

Asymmetric multiplayer games are rare in the local co-located space. Most require dedicated hardware or assume online play. Kaiju vs. Command targets the Jackbox-style social session: everyone in the same room, own devices, rounds short enough to swap seats and chase the high score.

The "managed retreat" framing — kaiju always win; the Commander scores by evacuating humans — creates a replayable loop with no binary win/lose frustration. Players want to beat their own evacuation record and the leaderboard, not just survive. The arcade aesthetic (1993 quarter-muncher, CRT phosphor, INSERT COIN flash) anchors the tone: this is a game you play loudly with other people, not a polished esports title.
