# Method 01 — Stakeholder Map

> Rough capture. Not polished. Updated during Method 1 scope conversations, 2026-06-23.

## Primary Players (in-session)

| Role | Count | What they want | Core tension |
|---|---|---|---|
| Incident Commander | 1 | Maximize humans evacuated before city falls | Triage: launch rockets vs. defend against kaiju |
| Kaiju player | 1–4 | Destroy the city base | Coordinate timing and targets with other kaiju |
| Spectator | 0–N | Watch, cheer, wait for next round | Eliminated kaiju players; also observers in the room |

## Fictional Stakeholders (in-game)

- **Humans on the ground** — the score metric. Evacuated via rockets the Commander triggers. Never seen directly; represented as an evacuation count.
- **The city** — the Commander's last line of defense. When it falls, the match ends.

## Real-World Audience

- **Social gamers in the same physical space** — the primary target. BYOD local co-located (Jackbox model).
- **First responders** — identified as a natural fit early in scoping. The "managed retreat" fantasy maps to incident command instincts. Not yet validated with actual first responders.
- **General arcade/strategy fans** — secondary. Anyone who enjoys asymmetric competitive games.

## Influence Map (rough)

- Commander has **indirect force** — dispatches assets with delay and uncertainty; triggers launches
- Kaiju players have **direct force** — move, attack, respawn freely; individually fragile
- Spectators have **social pressure** — their reactions shape the room energy (Killer Queen model)
